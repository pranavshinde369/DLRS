# DLRS Community Promotion Guide

**For Project Maintainers and Community Advocates**

This guide helps you promote DLRS effectively while maintaining ethical standards and building genuine community engagement.

---

## 🎯 Recommended Positioning

### English Positioning

**Primary**:
> DLRS is an open standard draft for privacy-first, consent-based digital life archives.

**Extended**:
> DLRS defines schemas, templates, consent records, governance rules, and validation tools for building structured digital-life repositories. It addresses privacy, consent, revocability, auditability, and ethical boundaries.

**Key Points**:
- ✅ Open standard draft (not a product)
- ✅ Privacy-first approach
- ✅ Consent-based framework
- ✅ For research and discussion
- ✅ Community-driven development

---

### 中文定位

**主要定位**：
> DLRS 是一个面向数字生命档案的开放标准草案，重点解决隐私优先、自愿授权、结构化存档、可撤回、可审计、Schema 校验和档案模板问题。

**扩展说明**：
> DLRS 定义了用于构建结构化数字生命仓库的 Schema、模板、授权记录、治理规则和验证工具。它解决隐私、授权、可撤回性、可审计性和伦理边界问题。

**关键点**：
- ✅ 开放标准草案（不是产品）
- ✅ 隐私优先方法
- ✅ 基于授权的框架
- ✅ 用于研究和讨论
- ✅ 社区驱动开发

## ❌ NOT Recommended Promotion Methods

### Don't Say:
- ❌ "复活人类" / "Resurrect humans"
- ❌ "完整克隆人格" / "Complete personality clone"
- ❌ "保证还原本人" / "Guaranteed to restore the person"
- ❌ "永生技术" / "Immortality technology"
- ❌ "AI 就是本人" / "AI equals the real person"
- ❌ "100% 合规保证" / "100% compliance guaranteed"
- ❌ "法律认可" / "Legally recognized"

### Don't Do:
- ❌ Buy fake stars or followers
- ❌ Spam communities with promotional posts
- ❌ Make exaggerated promises
- ❌ Claim endorsements that don't exist
- ❌ Ignore ethical concerns
- ❌ Pressure people to contribute
- ❌ Misrepresent the project's maturity

---

## ✅ Recommended Approach

- ✅ "Open standard draft for discussion"

- ✅ "For research and interoperability"

- ✅ Be transparent about limitations

## 📝 Community Post Templates

### Template 1: Hacker News / Reddit

**Title**: "DLRS: An open standard draft for privacy-first digital life archives (seeking feedback)"

**Body**:
```
Hi everyone,

I'm working on DLRS (Digital Life Repository Standard), an open standard draft for privacy-first, consent-based digital life archives.

**What it is:**
- JSON schemas for digital life records
- Consent and withdrawal models
- Privacy boundary definitions
- Validation tools and templates

**What it's NOT:**
- Not a product or service
- Not claiming to "resurrect" anyone
- Not making legal guarantees
- Not mature or production-ready

**Why I'm sharing:**
I'm looking for feedback on:
- Privacy model (what should never be in Git?)
- Consent framework (how to handle withdrawal?)
- Schema design (are the fields appropriate?)
- Ethical boundaries (what should be forbidden?)

**Current status:** v0.2 Draft (very early stage)

GitHub: https://github.com/Digital-Life-Repository-Standard/DLRS

I'd appreciate any thoughts, especially on privacy and ethics. What am I missing?
```

## 🌍 Platform-Specific Recommendations

### Hacker News
- **Best time**: Weekday mornings (US Pacific Time)
- **Approach**: Technical, honest, seeking feedback
- **Avoid**: Hype, marketing language, exaggeration
- **Engage**: Respond to all comments, acknowledge concerns
- **Follow up**: Share learnings and updates

### Reddit (r/programming, r/opensource, r/privacy)
- **Best time**: Weekday afternoons
- **Approach**: Detailed, transparent, community-focused
- **Avoid**: Self-promotion without value
- **Engage**: Answer questions, provide context
- **Follow up**: Post updates in comments, not new posts

### V2EX
- **Best time**: Weekday evenings (China time)
- **Approach**: Technical, practical, seeking Chinese community input
- **Avoid**: Over-promising, ignoring local regulations
- **Engage**: Respond in Chinese, address privacy concerns
- **Follow up**: Share progress, acknowledge feedback

### 知乎
- **Best time**: Weekends
- **Approach**: Educational, detailed, addressing Chinese context
- **Avoid**: Sensationalism, ignoring PIPL compliance
- **Engage**: Write detailed answers, cite sources
- **Follow up**: Update answer with community feedback

### 掘金 / 开源中国
- **Best time**: Weekday mornings (China time)
- **Approach**: Technical tutorial, code examples
- **Avoid**: Pure promotion without technical content
- **Engage**: Provide code samples, answer technical questions
- **Follow up**: Write follow-up articles on specific topics

### Twitter / X
- **Best time**: Weekday mornings and evenings
- **Approach**: Concise, visual, engaging
- **Avoid**: Thread spam, excessive hashtags
- **Engage**: Reply to comments, retweet feedback
- **Follow up**: Share milestones and learnings

## 📊 Metrics to Track

### Healthy Metrics:
- ✅ Genuine GitHub stars (organic growth)
- ✅ Quality issues and discussions
- ✅ Pull requests from community
- ✅ Constructive feedback
- ✅ Documentation improvements
- ✅ Translation contributions
- ✅ Thoughtful questions

### Unhealthy Metrics:
- ❌ Sudden star spikes (likely fake)
- ❌ No engagement despite high stars
- ❌ Spam issues or PRs
- ❌ Negative sentiment without response
- ❌ High bounce rate on docs
- ❌ No repeat contributors

---

## 🤝 Building Genuine Community

### Do:
1. **Respond to all feedback** (positive and negative)
2. **Acknowledge limitations** openly
3. **Credit contributors** publicly
4. **Share failures** and learnings
5. **Ask for help** when needed
6. **Collaborate** with related projects
7. **Host discussions** and Q&A sessions
8. **Document decisions** transparently
9. **Welcome criticism** constructively
10. **Celebrate milestones** together

### Don't:
1. ❌ Ignore negative feedback
2. ❌ Delete critical comments
3. ❌ Take credit for others' work
4. ❌ Hide mistakes or failures
5. ❌ Work in isolation
6. ❌ Compete with similar projects
7. ❌ Make decisions without discussion
8. ❌ Keep processes opaque
9. ❌ Dismiss concerns as "haters"
10. ❌ Celebrate prematurely

## 📅 Promotion Timeline

### Phase 1: Soft Launch (Current)
- Share in small, relevant communities
- Seek feedback from experts
- Iterate based on input
- Build core documentation
- Establish governance

### Phase 2: Community Building (Next 3 months)
- Write technical blog posts
- Create video tutorials
- Speak at meetups (if invited)
- Collaborate with related projects
- Grow contributor base

### Phase 3: Broader Outreach (6+ months)
- Submit to conferences (if accepted)
- Write academic papers (if appropriate)
- Engage with standards bodies
- Build partnerships
- Scale community programs
